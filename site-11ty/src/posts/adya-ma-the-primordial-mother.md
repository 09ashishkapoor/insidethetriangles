<p>In silence before creation’s dawn,<br>She sits,unseen, unknown, unborn;<br>Beyond Devaloka’s veiled gaze,<br>The origin none can trace or phrase.</p><p>Adya, Mother of mothers, root of all<br>From whom even Brahma and Vishnu fall;<br>Maheshwara bows at Your sacred feet,<br>O Adishakti, where all paths meet.</p><p>You are Sati’s fire and Sita’s grace,<br>The force that time cannot erase.<br>As Daksha’s daughters bloom and fade,<br>It is in Your womb the world is made.</p><p>In forests deep and temples old,<br>In every story once retold;<br>Your bhakta seeks with trembling tongue<br>From Bhakti only can You be won.</p><p>Even Hanuman with strength untold<br>With Ashta Siddhis bright as gold<br>Could not claim Your final prize:<br>Only Rama met Your eyes.</p><p>Silk or bark, wealth or woe<br>Keep Her crowned through ebb and flow.<br>O Maa Adya! Ancient One<br>Who births the moon and wakes the sun.</p><p>All forms are Yours, Kali fierce and Tara kind;<br>Yet formless ever in heart and mind.<br>Grand architect of Shakti Peethas’ flame,<br>With every breath we chant Your name.</p><p>No mantra bounds You to our call;<br>Only surrendered love enthralls.<br>Maa Adya! Source of cosmic play:<br>Guide this child upon Your way.</p><p>Jai Maa Adya!</p>
