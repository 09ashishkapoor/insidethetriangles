<p>A thread of moonlight, spun with grace, Matangi descends, a shadowed space. </p><p>Not bright and bold, a fiery hue, But whispers old.</p><p>She sits within the dust and stone, A silent watcher, utterly alone. </p><p>Not seeking light, nor sun's embrace, But holds the secrets of this place.</p><p>A bridge between the seen and unseen, Where past and present softly convene. </p><p>She pulls the threads of memory deep, And watches as the spirit sleeps.</p><p>The scent of earth, of moss and rain, Reflects the solace, and the pain. </p><p>A single leaf, a fading hue.</p><p>She doesn't judge, she doesn’t strive</p><p>She nutures the things that live Within the spaces, dark and low, Where truth resides, and shadows grow.</p><p>A weaver of the unseen thread, Of moments lost, and words unsaid. </p><p>Matangi, a quiet, watchful eye, A constant pulse beneath the sky.</p><p>She asks no questions, offers grace, A gentle stillness in this space. </p><p>To gather fragments, small and frail, And understand the breath of tale.</p><p>So gaze upon her, soft and slow, And feel the secrets she bestow. </p><p>For in her stillness, deep and vast, Lies Matangi, a shadow cast.</p><p></p><p>Jai Ma Adya , Jai Ma Matangi </p>
